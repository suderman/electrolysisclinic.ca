What is Electrolysis?

Electrolysis is a proven method of permanent hair removal developed in the 1800s. It is safe and works on any type of hair.

A very fine probe is inserted into the hair follicle, and delivers a tiny, split-second current. The current destroys the hair root. Hairs are treated using the Apilus SX-500 epilator.

Most hairs require at least three to five treatments for permanent removal, with two to three weeks between treatments.

Services

Commonly treated areas include:

  • upper-lip
  • chin
  • eyebrows
  • legs

Pregnant women, clients with skin disorders, or clients wishing to have hair removed from moles should seek physicians’ advice prior to treatment.

Treatment of ears, nostrils, and pubic area is not offered.
