What is Electrolysis?

Electrolysis was developed in the late 1800s, and is the only proven method of permanent hair removal. It is safe, relatively painless, and cost-effective. Electrolysis works on any type of hair; regardless of color, thickness, or texture.

The electrologist inserts a very fine probe into the hair follicle, and delivers a tiny, split-second current. The current destroys the hair root so that it can never produce another hair.

Although some hairs may be permanently removed after one treatment, stronger hairs may require more treatments to permanently destroy the hair root.

Services

Hair can safely be removed nearly anywhere on the body. Common areas treated include:

  • upper-lip
  • chin
  • cheeks
  • eyebrows
  • armpits
  • abdomen
  • back
  • legs
  • thighs

Hairs are individually treated and removed under a magnifying lamp. After treatment you will receive an application of cooling cream to the treated area.

Pregnant women, clients with skin disorders, or clients wishing to have hair removed from moles should seek physicians’ advice prior to treatment.

Treatment of ears, nostrils, and pubic area is not offered.
